How we use OKRs

We use OKRs to separate the results we want (Outcomes) from the work we do (Outputs).

🎯
Objectives — where are we going?
Inspirational, high-level goals aligned to our Strategic Pillars.
📊
Key Results — how do we measure success?
Our Outcomes. Hard, quantifiable business metrics — GOP %, eNPS, Cleanliness Score.
🚀
Initiatives — what projects are we running?
Our Outputs. Main projects or workstreams owned by a lead.
📅
Milestones — what are the project phases?
Major deliverables and deadlines that keep our Initiatives on track.
Example: putting it together
▸Objective (Guest): Deliver a distinct brand experience…
└▸Key Result: Raise Guest Cleanliness Score by +0.2
  └▸Initiative: Housekeeping Excellence Project
    └▸Milestone: Roll out Quality Standard training by Sept 1st
Remember: Finishing an Initiative on time is a successful output, but success is only achieved when the Key Result actually moves in the right direction — that's the outcome.
